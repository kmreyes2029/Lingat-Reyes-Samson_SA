Basic Facts and Geography

Zamboanga City is a highly urbanized city located in the southwestern tip of Mindanao, Philippines. It covers a large territory, ranging from at least 1400 to 1500km², making it one of the largest cities in the Philippines. The city bears the name “City of Flowers” which is believed to have come from the Malay word “jambangan” which means flower garden.

Population and Demographics

According to the 2024 census, the City of Zamboanga has a population of roughly a million people. As of the 2020 census, the population was recorded at 977,234. This shows that the city’s population has grown significantly over time. According to some demographic estimates, the age distribution in the city suggests a young population, with a median of approximately 22.3 years.

Culture, Language, and Society

The majority language locally is Chavacano (also known as Zamboangueño). Other commonly spoken languages include Cebuano, Tagalog, English, as well as various local and migrant languages. The city has a culturally diverse population: besides the Zamboangueños, there are communities of ethnolinguistic groups such as Yakan, Sama-Bajau, Tausug, Subanen, and others–partly due to migration from the nearby islands.

History and Significance

The area was an early center of trade among Chinese, Malay, and native Moro peoples as early as the 13th–14th century. The Spanish founded what became Zamboanga City in 1635, and established a fort (Fort Pilar) for defense – a historical site that remains until today. Over the years, the city played a huge role as a gateway to the Sulu Archipelago and as a center of trade and defense during the colonial times. In modern times, the city is recognized as the commercial and industrial center of the wider region of Zamboanga Peninsula.
